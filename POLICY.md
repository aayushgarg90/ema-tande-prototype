# Corporate Travel & Expense Policy (Sample)

This is the policy the Expense Agent checks every report against before it
reaches a manager. Only genuine exceptions are surfaced for a decision —
everything else auto-clears with no one having to look at it.

## Airfare — GL 6010
- Economy/coach class for flights under 6 hours; premium economy is fine for
  anything longer.
- No fixed dollar cap, but flag a single domestic fare over **$900** 

## Lodging — GL 6020
- Nightly rate should stay at or below **$380/night** in major metro
  destinations, **$280/night** elsewhere.
- Flag any night that exceeds the applicable cap.

## Ground transport — GL 6030
- Rideshare, taxi, parking, and rental cars are all in-policy by default.
- Flag a single ground-transport charge over **$100** if nothing about it
  (long airport transfer, multi-day rental, etc.) explains the amount.

## Meals — GL 6040
- Meals are covered up to **$75 per person, per meal**, tip included.
- A meal over $75 is flagged **unless** it's reclassified as Client
  entertainment (GL 6045) with attendees documented.

## Client entertainment — GL 6045
- Meals or activities with clients/prospects are covered above the standard
  meal limit, but the attendees (names, or a headcount + company) must be
  documented.
- Flag anything coded as client entertainment with no attendees listed.

## Other — GL 6050
- Flag anything that doesn't clearly fit the categories above, or that looks
  unusually high for what it's described as (e.g. over **$150** with no
  context explaining it).

## General rules for the agent
- Only flag a *genuine* policy exception — being the largest line item on a
  report is not, by itself, a reason to flag something.
- A report with zero flagged items should be treated as fully auto-cleared
  and never need a manager's attention.
- Every flag needs a one-sentence reason a manager could read and immediately
  understand — cite the specific rule and the specific number.
